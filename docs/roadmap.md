# Roadmap

> Where I'm going, and why it matters.

<br/>

---

## Near Term — Next 6 Months

**Theme:** Ship, learn, and build momentum.

- [ ] **Ship Project November v1** — Launch the first production version of the productivity platform with core habit tracking, fitness logging, and dashboard features.
- [ ] **Scale Zero Point Studios** — Take on more clients, refine the studio workflow, and build a portfolio of shipped products.
- [ ] **Strengthen ML & LLM Engineering** — Deepen practical skills in machine learning pipelines and large language model integration for production use cases.
- [ ] **Open Source Contribution** — Make at least one meaningful contribution to a project I use in production.
- [ ] **System Design Fundamentals** — Study distributed systems, database design, and scalability patterns.

---

## Mid Term — 1 to 2 Years

**Theme:** Build real things. Grow technical depth.

- [ ] **Launch AI-Powered Products** — Build and ship at least one product that uses AI as a core feature, not a gimmick.
- [ ] **Grow Zero Point Studios** — Establish the studio as a recognized name for high-quality digital product development.
- [ ] **Competitive Programming** — Build strong profiles on LeetCode, Codeforces, and other platforms. Strengthen algorithmic thinking.
- [ ] **Graduate School Preparation** — Research programs, prepare applications, and build the academic and project portfolio needed for top CS programs.
- [ ] **Cloud Architecture** — Gain practical experience with cloud-native architectures, containerization, and infrastructure-as-code.

---

## Long Term — 3 to 5 Years

**Theme:** Depth. Impact. Craft.

- [ ] **Graduate Studies** — Pursue an MS or research-focused program in Computer Science and AI. Aspiring target: University of Toronto.
- [ ] **Build a Startup** — Found a company around a problem worth solving, with technology that makes a genuine difference.
- [ ] **Exceptional Software Engineering** — Become the kind of engineer who can architect, build, and ship complex systems with confidence.
- [ ] **Foundational Open Source** — Contribute to open source projects that have broad impact — frameworks, tools, or infrastructure.
- [ ] **Research** — Publish work at the intersection of AI, product engineering, and human-centered design.

---

## Guiding Principles

These are the constraints I use to evaluate decisions:

1. **Build things that matter.** Time is finite. Work on problems worth solving.
2. **Depth over breadth.** It's better to be excellent at a few things than mediocre at many.
3. **Ship early, iterate often.** Perfect is the enemy of done. Learn from users, not assumptions.
4. **Stay technical.** Titles change. Technical skills compound.
5. **Think long term.** Optimize for the 10-year outcome, not the 10-day result.
